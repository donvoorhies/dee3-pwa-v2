To best explain what a PWA ("Progressive Web App") is, I'll hand that over to the happy, knowledgable folks at Google, who can probably can explain this in a more engaging, interesting and non-nerdy way, better than I can! You can read more about what PWAs are, by reading this article...

"Installation & How to use":

Android-users:

You've probably accessed this PWA from some URL-link or QR-code, and now have it running in your mobile device's web browser. If you're using an Android-based mobile-device, chances are that you've already seen/reacted to a push-message suggesting, if you should add this to your startscreen; if you've already done so: good for you! Now close your web browser, find the "dee3"-icon somewhere on your mobile device's startscreen (or under your: "Applications"-list), tap on the icon, and enjoy the sets available via this PWA! Otherwise, if you've missed out on the aforementioned push-message: don't worry! Just push the menu in the upper right-hand corner of your browser (not the "hamburger"-icon of this PWA, but the one with the three dots), from the menu that appears choose: "Add to startscreen", and you're good to go!

iOS-users - some additional words:

Currently, iOS-browsers - AFAIK - do not support the so-called "Service-Worker"-scripts (although Apple's soon going to change this!); but in the meantime: Like the Android-users, you too have probably accessed this PWA from some URL-link or QR-code, and now have it running in your mobile device's web browser! Tap on the "Add to StartScreen"-icon (the square with the arrow pointing upwards, located down below in the middle of the browser), and the PWA will then be installed on your iOS-device! Now close your Safari-mobile browser, find the "dee3"-icon somewhere on your mobile device's startscreen (or under your: "Applications"-list), tap on the icon, and you too can now enjoy the sets available via this PWA!
